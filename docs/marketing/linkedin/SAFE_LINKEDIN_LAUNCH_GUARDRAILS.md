# SAFE LinkedIn Launch Guardrails — May/June 2026

> Companion to `LINKEDIN_PLAYBOOK.md`, `LINKEDIN_AGENT_KB.md`, and the May 18 launch calendar.
> Purpose: make the launch calendar publishable without weakening SAFE through weak sourcing, overclaims, or unsustainable cadence.

Last updated: 2026-05-15

---

## 1. Launch Mode

The May 18 to June 13 calendar is a launch offensive, not the permanent LinkedIn operating rhythm.

- Target cadence: 5 posts/week from Monday to Friday, 1 newsletter on Saturday, 1 carousel on Wednesday.
- Fallback cadence: if the 5 week-1 posts are not fully drafted, sourced, reviewed, and scheduled by Sunday, May 17, publish Monday/Wednesday/Friday plus the Saturday newsletter.
- Quality rule: one excellent post that starts conversations is better than filling the calendar with a weak post.
- Review trigger: after 14 days, decide whether to keep 5 posts/week or step down to 3 posts/week based on qualified conversations, profile visits, and demos booked.

The older internal marketing guidance used 2 posts/week. Treat that as the baseline operating cadence. Treat the May/June plan as a time-boxed launch sprint.

## 2. CTA Rules

Each post gets one primary conversion job.

| Post type | Primary CTA | Use when | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Founder story | Follow/profile view/conversation | Identity, origin, build in public | Audit pitch too early |
| Segment A pain | `DM AUDIT` | Established solo/small firm, measurable admin or billing leakage | QUIZ CTA |
| Segment B launch | `DM SOLO` or quiz | Young lawyer thinking about solo practice | AUDIT CTA unless already established |
| Contrarian opinion | Comment/conversation/newsletter | Broad debate or framing shift | Hard demo pitch |
| Newsletter | Subscribe or soft demo CTA | Long-form explanation or manifesto | Multiple competing links in post body |

Hard rule: do not push the QUIZ publicly until the quiz form, scoring logic, email capture, report delivery, and follow-up email are tested end to end.

## 3. Safe Claim Language

Use specific, defensible wording. Do not turn an insight into an absolute claim.

| Risky phrasing | Safer phrasing |
|---|---|
| Tous les cabinets qui ont QBO bricolent un Excel parallèle. | Beaucoup de cabinets que j'observe finissent avec un Excel parallèle. |
| Les outils US ne respectent pas le Barreau / la Loi 25. | Les outils génériques ne sont pas conçus autour des contraintes Barreau / Loi 25. |
| Le vertical est la seule réponse. | Le vertical est, selon moi, la réponse la plus sérieuse à ce type de contrainte métier. |
| Scandale silencieux. | À utiliser seulement si le post a déjà posé une preuve chiffrée solide. |
| SAFE rend le cabinet 100 % conforme. | SAFE aide à structurer les workflows autour des obligations applicables; la conformité finale reste à valider par le cabinet. |
| Le cabinet ne facture jamais à son plein potentiel. | Dans l'expérience fondatrice, le cabinet laissait régulièrement du temps facturable sur la table. |

Barreau/Loi 25 rule: never claim legal compliance as a guaranteed product outcome in a public post. Frame SAFE as infrastructure, workflow, documentation, and risk reduction.

## 4. Anti-Fiction Filter

Every post must pass this filter before drafting and again before publication.

- No invented scenes, places, timestamps, quantities, or dialogue.
- Narrative details are allowed only if Jérémie personally lived them or can attribute them to a real interview/testimonial.
- If the story material is thin, use a sourced data post, a build-in-public note, or an interview-based post.
- If a post says "j'ai vu", "une juge m'a confirmé", or "deux avocates m'ont dit", keep the detail exactly within what is already known.
- Do not add dramatic color to make the story more cinematic.

Approved recurring founder-story facts:

- Jérémie held the books for a family-law lawyer.
- QuickBooks was refused because it was too complex and poorly adapted to the practice.
- The workflow relied on Excel, paper time sheets, and emails.
- One invoice could take around 30 minutes.
- There was delay and forgotten billable time.
- A judge confirmed the pattern was not isolated.
- Two other lawyers described a similar Excel-based reality.
- SAFE has been built since August 2025 by Jérémie as a solo builder.

## 6. Week-1 Acceptance Criteria

Before scheduling week 1, each asset must satisfy:

- Voice: short, concrete, operator-led, no generic AI polish.
- CTA: one clear primary action.
- Evidence: every number appears in `SAFE_LINKEDIN_SOURCE_LEDGER.md`.
- Legal/commercial restraint: no unsupported compliance or ROI guarantee.
- Anti-fiction: no invented narrative detail.
- Link readiness: audit, quiz, newsletter, and booking links tested if mentioned.

Week-1 success metrics:

- Primary: qualified comments, profile visits, DM conversations, newsletter signups.
- Secondary: views, likes, impressions.
- Ignore: generic applause that does not create ICP conversation.

---

## 7. 7-Day and 14-Day Reviews

Day 7 review:

- Count profile visits, qualified comments, DM starts, newsletter signups, and demo intent.
- Identify the top 2 posts by qualified conversation, not likes.
- Keep the CTA that produced the clearest ICP replies.

Day 14 review:

- If drafting quality or sourcing quality drops, reduce to 3 posts/week.
- If DMs are active but not qualified, tighten hooks toward Segment A pain.
- If views are high but DMs are low, make CTAs more specific and reduce broad founder-content.
- If Segment B engages but the quiz is not ready, redirect to newsletter instead of promising quiz delivery.
